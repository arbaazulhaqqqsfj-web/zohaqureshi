From Hyderabad to the world

A young athlete with
a fearless ambition.

Zoha Qureshi is one of India’s rising junior tennis players—known for her powerful game, competitive toughness and relentless work ethic.

Born and raised in Hyderabad, Zoha began playing at just three to four years old. Today, she competes for India internationally with one clear dream: to become world No. 1.

Where it began

Her story

Sport was always
part of the family.

Zoha’s sporting life began early. Alongside her brothers and sisters, she explored gymnastics and skating before tennis became her calling. Her father, Abrar ul Haq Qureshi, recognised her potential when a tennis Academy opened and helped place a racquet in her hands.

Her mother, Safura, and grandfather, Abdul Haq, have been constant sources of strength. So has a deeply sporting family, her sister Fatima is an international player; Arbaaz and Zunaira have competed nationally,

“The dream is simple: keep working, keep growing, and become world No. 1.”

Behind every point

A father’s
quiet belief.

Behind every trophy is a journey few people see. Zoha’s father, Abrar ul Haq Qureshi, recognised her potential when she was still very young—and has stood beside her through the early mornings, long journeys, difficult losses and demanding tournament schedules ever since.

His dedication has often meant putting her dream before his own comfort, making sacrifices quietly and asking for nothing in return. Through every challenge, he has given Zoha something greater than opportunity: the belief that her biggest dream is worth pursuing.

Some victories are lifted on court. Others are built quietly, day after day, by the people who never stop believing.
